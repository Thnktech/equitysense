🚀 Getting Started Guide

  1. API Key Setup: To pull live data, you need a free API key from Alpha Vantage. Enter it in the sidebar and click "Connect". Your key is saved locally in your browser session.
  2. Building Your Portfolio: Navigate to the Portfolio tab. Click "Add Position". You must enter the Ticker Symbol (e.g., AAPL), your average buy price, and the number of shares.
  3. Updating Prices: The app does not auto-refresh to save your API limits. Click the "Update All Prices" button in the Portfolio header to fetch the latest market data.

🧠 Deep Dive: Features Explained

1. Investor Profile & Diagnostic

This is the brain of the application. The 10-question analysis determines your "Investor Type" (e.g., Compounder, Risk Minimizer). This profile dictates your Maximum Allocation Limits and Risk Tolerance Weights used in the Buy/Sell tabs.

2. The "Firewall" Logic

The firewall is a rules-based scoring engine (0-100) that ignores price action and focuses on business health. It evaluates:

  • Growth (30%): Is Revenue and EPS growing year-over-year? (>10% is ideal).
  • Profitability (30%): Is ROE > 15%? Are Operating Margins expanding?
  • Safety (20%): Is Debt-to-Equity < 0.5? (Low debt is safer).
  • Trend (20%): Is the price above the 200-Day Moving Average? (Bullish trend).

3. Buy Now? (Opportunity Scanner)

This tab finds the highest quality stocks in your portfolio that are currently underweight.

Example: If you are a "Concentrator" (allowed 25% per stock) and hold Google (Score 90) at only 5% allocation, this tab will signal "Add Aggressively".

4. Sell Now? (Risk Scanner)

This tab highlights two types of sell signals:

  • Fundamental Decay: Stocks with Scores < 50 (Slowing growth, high debt).
  • Portfolio Risk: Stocks that are "Overweight" based on your profile (e.g., a "Diversifier" holding 30% in one stock will get a "Trim" alert).
